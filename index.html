JIM'S WEB

MENTORSHIP PROGRAM IN MEMORY OF JIM WALROD

8-Ball Community has created the Jim’s Web mentorship program to keep this spirit alive. The program will connect established arts professionals with emerging creatives to help them realize creative projects around a theme, in fields including fashion design, fine art, architecture/interiors, product design, publishing, and photography. The inaugural theme for Jim’s Web is “community,” in honor of Jim’s ability to bring people together. Six mentorships -one in each discipline- will be awarded annually. Awardees will receive a grant of $3,000 and access to their mentor’s studio or office space to realize their project over a six month period. The projects will be showcased in a group exhibition at the end of the program.

We are currently seeking mentors to contribute their expertise to one mentee over a six month period. Mentors will need to provide their mentee access to their studio or office twice a month throughout the six month mentorship period (between February and August 2019) and advise the mentee on their proposed project.

ABOUT JIM’S WEB

im’s Web is a mentorship program that connects emerging creatives with creative professionals who can advise them on a project.

Six mentorships will be awarded each year, one in each of the following disciplines:

  • Architecture / Interiors
  • Fashion Design
  • Fine Art
  • Product Design
  • Photography
  • Publishing

Each awardee will receive a grant of $3,000 and access to to their mentor’s studio space over the course of the six month term. The completed projects will be showcased in a group exhibition.

ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS

No age requirement.

Participants must be able and available to complete their project between February and August 2019. Mentorship projects must be completed by September 1st.

APPLICATION PROCESS

Candidates interested in participating as a mentee must complete the JIM’S WEB APPLICATION FORM by DATE.

In addition to completing the form, applicants must submit up to 6 samples of past work (with explanations if needed) as a single PDF.
